# Unearned Bounty Systems Roadmap From USI Research

Date: 2026-05-22

This document translates Unnamed Space Idle system research into Unearned Bounty-native systems that fit the visual design bible: toy-theater sea adventure, captain's desk progression, pushed cartoon silhouettes, mystical but operational tools, and bright combat with quiet controls.

Use this as a todo list and design brief. It is not a promise to implement every system.

## Translation Rules

- Every system should feel like a physical captain's desk object, ship fitting, port facility, officer practice, or charted sea phenomenon.
- Magic must be operational: bottled, slotted, burned, charted, commissioned, stamped, or rigged.
- System icons need silhouette-first reads at small size.
- New mechanics should visibly affect the sea, ship, projectiles, route, or Captain's Desk.
- Solved friction should later become automated, compressed, or retired.
- Avoid generic sci-fi resource translation. Prefer pirate materials, relic objects, sea routes, port tools, infamy, and voyage language.

## System Unfolding Plan

USI's useful pattern is not only "unlock a tab at a sector." It introduces a tab, gives it one clear job, then later adds cross-system hooks so older tabs become part of newer solutions. UB should follow that rhythm: every new Captain's Desk object appears with a narrow purpose, then later passages make it interlock with routes, Return to Port, boss milestones, or another desk object.

Target passages are pacing targets, not hard promises. "Passage" here means the player-facing sector clear or first Return to Port after that clear.

| Target Passage | Unlock / Expansion | First Job | Later Interconnects |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Start | Sea Sectors + Arsenal | Push distance, sink visible ships, spend Salvage on weapon/hull. | Arsenal milestones later read ships sunk, route tags, Research, and Shipwright fittings. |
| Passage 1 clear | Prestige planning | Explain Return to Port, reset contract, and next goal. | Prestige becomes the place for loadout, starting-sector charts, port bonuses, and automation templates. |
| Passage 2 clear + Return | Muster | Turn combat into crew allocation between Gunnery and Seamanship. | Officers later preserve/catch up discipline records; Research can unlock extra Muster branches. |
| Passage 3 clear | Targeting Orders | Make visible fleets mechanically honest with Focus/Suppression/Scatter. | Gunnery Research unlocks more priorities; Captain's Orders automates doctrine rules. |
| Passage 4 clear | Stormheart Furnace | Convert rare Ether Brine into temporary Storm Power boosts. | Occult Research improves Brine; Shipwright builds boost fittings; Storm Contracts consume/charge through Stormheart. |
| Passage 5 clear or first Black Reef branch | Shipwright's Bench | Craft fittings over time and establish Blueprint Mastery. | Shipwrighting Research speeds crafting; Port Facilities produce materials; modules become Return to Port loadout pieces. |
| Passage 6 clear | Admiralty Research | Branch progress from kills, with one focused branch. | Route tags weight research; Research unlocks route previews, module slots, relic sockets, and automation. |

| Passage 15 clear | Port Facilities seed | First tiny port grid that produces persistent bonuses after Return. | Facilities support Arsenal, Shipwright, Research, Stormheart, and Officers through named buildings. |
| Passage 18 clear | Captain's Trials | Focused rule-bending runs that teach one system at a time. | Trial completions add permanent bonuses and become Captain's Ledger requirements. |
| Passage 20 clear | Officers | Character specialization after the main machine is readable. | Officers preserve/catch up Muster-like disciplines, improve automation rules, and specialize Shipwright/Research/Contracts. |
| Passage 25 clear | Captain's Orders | Automate solved chores: targeting, boost rules, crafting queues, research focus. | Orders become loadout templates that combine Prestige, Shipwright modules, Research focus, and route choices. |

| Passage 40+ | Flagship Phase seed | Bigger flagship/fleet layer, not just higher numbers. | Compresses solved early passages and reuses Arsenal, Officers, Research, Stormheart, and Shipwright in larger forms. |

### Unlock Philosophy

- A new tab should unlock only when the player has a reason to care about its first job.
- First unlocks should be small: one socket, one craft slot, one boost, one focus choice, one trial type.
- Later passages should expand existing tabs before adding new ones, especially after Passage 10.
- Route branches should not only be harder; they should point at systems. Black Reef leans Shipwrighting/Gunnery, Storm Line leans Navigation/Occult, and Trade Wind remains the baseline path.
- Return to Port should become the ritual that makes cross-system decisions readable: ship/weapon/defense/utility, starting passage, active modules, relic loadout, and automation template.
- Captain's Orders should arrive after repetition is visible, not before the player understands what is being automated.

## 1. Sea Sectors

### Design Fit

This is the main horizon-opening system. It should feel like charting dangerous water, not clearing a checklist. The visual design bible wants a living ocean lane and captain's desk charts; sectors are the chart spine.

### Mechanical Brief

- Each sector has:
  - sector number
  - route tag
  - distance goal
  - enemy fleet table
  - boss
  - first-clear unlocks
  - route rewards/densities
- Holding position farms waves at current distance.
- Forward course advances distance toward the boss.
- Boss clear advances the sector and may unlock systems.

### Visual / UI Language

- Desk object: chart strip, compass pins, route seals.
- Combat tell: horizon marker, boss silhouette, route color accents.
- Resource icon style: charts use Saltglass Teal, boss/infamy seals use Signal Red plus Bounty Gold.

### Todo

- Done in prototype: `data/definitions/sectors.json` exists for authored early sectors, with generated fallback sectors after that.
- Done in prototype: track `sector`, route distance, boss phase, boss first-clears, route names, route tags, and first-clear unlock metadata.
- Done in prototype: player-facing "sector" language has replaced the major "lane" labels. Legacy lane ids remain only for migration and authored boss compatibility.
- Done in prototype: track ships sunk this sector, best ships sunk for the sector, and lifetime ships sunk so farming while Held becomes a measurable USI-style preparation loop.
- Next: add a small sector chart panel once route branches need player choice.

### Research Prompts

- What does a 100-sector pirate route chart look like when grouped into seas of 10?
- Which sectors should branch into harder routes, and where do those branches rejoin?
- What should route density mean: salvage, relics, research, materials, crew XP, or enemy types?

### Design Defaults

- Group sectors into seas/regions of 10 by default. This matches the visual bible's route/region language and makes 100 sectors easier to author.
- Route choice should be a chart/map decision at branch points. Course controls remain moment-to-moment movement controls, not route selection.
- Holding position freezes sector distance by default and continues spawning waves. Add small drift only if the combat scene needs more life later.

## 2. Arsenal

### Design Fit

Arsenal is the first "working captain's desk" system: a quiet panel that makes the bright sea change. It should feel like buying refits, not pressing abstract DPS buttons.

### Mechanical Brief

- Upgrade currently equipped weapon and hull/defense.
- No basic level cap.
- Low scaling, frequent buys.
- Every 5 levels grants a milestone.
- Weapon upgrades increase impact, not firing spam.

### Visual / UI Language

- Desk object: brass-edged refit cards, gunpowder ledger, hull plank diagrams.
- Combat tells: thicker smoke, stronger recoil, bigger splashes, heavier hit pause.
- Avoid: old "weapon core/counters" explanatory blocks in Arsenal; selection belongs in Return to Port.

### Todo

- Done in prototype: milestone choice UI appears on reached every-5 levels.
- Done in prototype: weapon upgrade levels visibly affect smoke, shot weight, and impact splash.
- Done in prototype: Long Nine milestones include Prize Ledger, a behavior-flavored choice that trades raw damage for a growing salvage pickup bonus from ships sunk this sector.
- Later: add hull/defense family selection in Return to Port.

### Research Prompts

- What milestone styles in USI Core create meaningful build identity rather than raw stat growth?
- Which UB weapon families should be first: Long Nines, Harpoons, Firepots, Chainshot, Shrine Lantern?

### Design Defaults

- Every 5th level is sometimes a behavior choice, not always a stat multiplier. Direct multipliers remain the simplest baseline milestone.
- Arsenal auto-buy belongs in Captain's Orders / AI-style quality-of-life automation, similar to USI.

## 3. Return To Port

### Design Fit

This is prestige as a pirate ritual: the ship limps back, the desk resets to a new charter, and the captain picks a new loadout. It should feel like raising new colors, not wiping progress.

### Mechanical Brief

- Reset run resources and Arsenal levels.
- Preserve boss first clears, system unlocks, long-term mastery.
- Let player select ship, weapon, defense, utility.
- Unlock new systems after specific boss clears plus Return to Port.
- Once Return to Port is unlocked, it stays available. It does not require a fresh boss clear every voyage.

### Visual / UI Language

- Desk object: harbor charter, wax seals, ship silhouette cards, stamped warnings.
- Infamy motif: player's ship appears on notices as their own bounty grows.

### Todo

- Move loadout info fully into Return to Port / Prestige.
- Add ship, weapon, defense, utility cards.
- Add "what resets / what persists" preview.
- Add starting-sector chart unlock later.

### Research Prompts

- How does USI make prestige feel like reconfiguration rather than punishment?
- What information does a player need before choosing a ship/weapon/defense/utility?

### Design Defaults

- Return to Port is available after the first unlock.
- Salvage resets.
- Doubloons persist.
- Ether Brine resets as the UB equivalent of USI Void Matter.
- Crafted module state resets enough to make voyages distinct, but Blueprint Mastery / Standing Supply restores mastered tiers.
- Relic Attunement persists; active relic loadout can be reselected per voyage.

## 6. Stormheart Furnace

### Design Fit

USI has Void Matter as a resetting second currency used by Reactor and Compute-related power. UB should have an equivalent because the design bible already supports operational magic: bottled, burned, charted, and rigged. Stormheart Furnace turns strange sea fuel into temporary voyage power.

### Mechanical Brief

- Enemies and routes drop Ether Brine.
- Ether Brine resets on Return to Port.
- The Furnace converts Ether Brine into Storm Power.
- Storm Power runs limited concurrent boosts.
- Running more boosts than the limit drains power faster.
- Captain's Orders can automate boost rules later.

### Boost Examples

- Thunder Broadside: damage.
- Saltward Bloom: hull recovery / ward strength.
- Fair Wind: sector distance gain.
- Deep Salvage: salvage and rare drop chance.
- Lantern Wake: research and chart gain.
- Maelstrom Anchor: Storm Contract charge speed.

### Visual / UI Language

- Desk object: bottled storm brine, copper furnace gauge, glass pressure lens.
- Combat tell: boost-specific wake color, cannon lightning, saltward shimmer, or brighter salvage nets.
- Resource color: Storm Cyan / Saltglass Teal, with Ritual Violet only for occult variants.

### Todo

- Done in prototype: Ether Brine is a run-resetting rare kill pickup.
- Done in prototype: Storm Power is a run-resetting derived resource made by burning Ether Brine.
- Done in prototype: Thunder Broadside, Fair Wind, and Deep Salvage boosts are toggleable and drain Storm Power.
- Done in prototype: concurrent boost limit and over-limit drain are represented.
- Next: add stronger visual combat tells for each active boost.

### Research Prompts

- Which USI Reactor rules create useful choices without becoming micromanagement?
- What is the minimum boost UI that shows power income, drain, active boosts, and over-limit drain?

### Design Defaults

- Ether Brine resets like USI Void Matter.
- Stormheart should unlock after the player understands Arsenal and sector bosses, likely after Shipwright or Research unless balance needs it earlier.
- Boosts should be toggleable but eventually automatable through Captain's Orders.
